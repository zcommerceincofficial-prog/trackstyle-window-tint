FAQ

COMMON
QUESTIONS.

What's the difference between Carbon and Ceramic tint?
Both block 99% of UV and give you a dark, clean appearance. The main difference is heat rejection. Ceramic uses nano-ceramic technology to reject up to 95% of infrared heat — the heat you feel through glass — while Carbon film offers solid protection at a lower price point. In Phoenix, the upgrade to Ceramic is often worth it.
How long does installation take?
Most passenger sedans and coupes take 1–2 hours. Larger SUVs, trucks, or vehicles with complex rear window shapes may take a bit longer. We'll give you an accurate time estimate when you book. Most customers drop off and pick up same day.
Will tint affect my phone signal, GPS, or radio?
No. Both SunTek Evolve Carbon and Ceramic films are non-metallic, which means zero interference with your phone, GPS, toll transponder, or radio signals. This is one of the key advantages of the SunTek Evolve line over older metallic films.
What's legal for window tint in Arizona?
Arizona law requires front side windows to allow at least 33% of light in (33% VLT minimum). Rear side windows and the rear windshield can be any darkness. We always make sure front windows are installed to stay within legal limits — and we'll walk you through your options before we start.
How long before I can roll my windows down?
We recommend waiting 3–5 days before rolling your windows down after installation. This gives the adhesive time to cure fully. In Phoenix heat, curing is typically faster than in colder climates. During that window, avoid washing your car and leave the windows up.
Is tint covered under a warranty?
Yes. SunTek Evolve film is backed by a manufacturer warranty against bubbling, peeling, fading, and delamination. Ask us about warranty coverage when you book — it varies by film tier.
